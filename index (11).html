Standing behind the number

We don't replace the appraiser. We hand them measured evidence.

Parallax is measurement infrastructure, not an automated valuation model. The reconstruction produces an auditable workfile; a state-licensed appraiser reviews it, applies judgment, and signs the report. That keeps the value defensible — and keeps everyone's license intact.

01

Measured to standard

Gross living area is taken off the 3D model to the ANSI Z765 residential standard — the same rulebook a tape measure is supposed to follow, applied without the transcription error.

02

Appraiser independence, intact

The licensed appraiser owns the opinion of value. Parallax never sets the number — it supplies the geometry — so appraiser-independence rules are preserved, not bent.

03

Built for the workfile

Every report is UAD 3.6-native on Fannie and Freddie forms, with the measured-area exhibit attached — structured to support a USPAP-compliant workfile a reviewer can defend.

04

Auditable end to end

Every dimension traces to a point in the scan. Reopen any figure and see where it came from — no re-keyed numbers, no black box between capture and value.

The evidence

On a 44-home sample, the county record was wrong 80% of the time.

Each point is one property — county-record square footage across the bottom, Parallax's measured GLA up the side. If records were right, every point would land on the line. They don't, and they miss in one direction: public data under-reports living area by a median of 2.6%. That error flows silently into every comp built on top of it.
